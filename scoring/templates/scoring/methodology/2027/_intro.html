Summary

This is the draft methodology for the 2027 Council Climate Action Scorecards. This lays out how we'll be marking and scoring all UK councils for a third time on the actions they're taking towards net zero. The third edition of Council Climate Action Scorecard results will be released in Spring 2027.

As previously, our assessment will include 93 questions across 7 different sections. The majority of changes to the methodology between the 2025 and 2027 Action Scorecards have been to add clarifications. To find out more about how we created the complete methodology in 2022 and why questions or topics were or were not included in the overall methodology, check out our blog series on the methodology here.

There is one entirely new question in the methodology:

  • Has the council set limits on upfront embodied emissions for new build developments? (Question 4b. in Planning and Land Use)

A new tier has been introduced to the question looking at Climate Action Plans (Question 2a in Collaboration and Engagement). The new question tier is:

  • Does the council include climate adaptation as part of the Climate Action Plan or has it published a separate Climate Adaptation Plan that covers the area and includes references to SMART targets?

One question has been removed:

  • Does the council’s medium term financial plan include the council’s net zero target and make tackling the climate emergency one of its main priorities? (Question 1b in Governance and Finance).

This was due to Medium Term Financial Plans often not being publicly available and difficult to compare against other councils. The information gathered for this question was often identical or similar to Question 1a.

We have also taken the decision not to assess all district councils in the 2027 Scorecards due to local government reorganisation. You can read more about it in the ‘New or Councils that have changed structures’ section below.

We created this updated criteria through extensive research and consultation with council staff, councillors, campaigners and other organisations. To understand what action we will be scoring and the question weightings, please read the complete methodology below.

Methodology Review

Following the publication of the 2025 Council Climate Action Scorecards in June 2025, we have conducted a detailed review of the Action Scorecards methodology. We conducted this review in order to improve and clarify the questions, thereby ensuring the Action Scorecards are relevant, accurate and useful for residents and councils. This review happened between January and March 2026. Climate Emergency UK reviewed the questions and reflected on feedback from the sector, councils and campaigners on the 2025 Scorecards methodology. We held online roundtables for each section, with over 100 councillors, council officers, sector organisations, campaigners and Scorecards volunteers attending. Participants were also sent a short feedback form to provide comment on the suggested changes.

Following the roundtables, all the suggested changes to the methodology, including any new questions, criteria changes or clarifications were reviewed in detail by the 2027 Action Scorecards Advisory Group.
